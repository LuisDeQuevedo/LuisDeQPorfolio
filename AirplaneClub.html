Columbia Airplane Club

This year, we have decided to go with a much lighter design as compared to last year. To that end, we will go with a monokote wing design. Monokote is a light-weight shrink wrap film that can be adhered to balsa wood to form complex shapes. In previous years, the skin of the plan, including the wings, was wrapped in foam which is much, much heavier.

Our design this year will also have a dihedral design, which means that the wings are at a positive angle relative to the horizontal axis. Dihedral wings will give our plane greater roll stability and control.

This year, I have worked on the central component that couples the fuselage and the wings together. As part of this design, the wings are mounted at an angle to give them a dihedral angle. This cross-bar element is currently being iterated through to make it lighter and have the functionality of rotating the wings for easier transportation.

As the lead for the structure subteam, I have been developing an early prototype assembly that contains all the basic elements for a test flight. This assembly, shown below, contains my cross-bar design as well as part from the rest of the structures subteam.
